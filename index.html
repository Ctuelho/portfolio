Gomma

Game made for Ludum Dare 50, by myself only. My goals with this game were to create a sensation of solitude and slow-passing time. The player is alone in a desolate place and the only company they have is a giant slow-moving grub, that will, eventually, pass, leaving the player alone again. The grub moves so slow that the player has time to cultivate plants to feed it, but also to stop it from moving while it is busy eating, delaying its departure. I was responsible for:

  • Game Design - I designed the whole game: world, characters, and the core mechanics
  • I programmed the game
  • Art - I made the sprites, animations, and visual effects
  • I composed the title screen theme song

Hidden Lore

In my childhood, I was fascinated by RPG and was always creating campaigns and characters. Unfortunately, I did not get to play much, because I almost always got to be the game master :'(. But I really enjoyed creating the histories and worlds.

I also used to make a lot of board games to play with my friends. We really liked rolling dies to walk and discover the events on the board! I even made some card games, card by card! I remember I used to color the cards with Hydrocolor!

During college, I had the opportunity to participate in a scientific initiation where the theme was Art and Technology in Ecoarte. I was responsible for implementing interactive graphics applications, with Arduino and Processing, that compounded the piece of digital art. That was my first contact with computer graphics.

I also participated in a scientific initiation where we developed an experimental game to teach Physics to children. I had to implement some physics functions, such as buoyancy, and some AI, such as the flocking algorithm. That was my first contact with Unity and C#, since then we live as a happy threesome.
